Tiny lab-grown plants could save the desert

  1. Anita Antoninka dribbles a stream of water over tiny black dots on a small patch of Arizona desert.
  2. Although the soil at her feet appears dusty and lifeless, the ecologist promises there’s hidden life there
  3. The dots unfurl into a sumptuous blanket of teeny, dark-green leaves: moss.
  4. Each moss is smaller than a pencil eraser, and the sudden appearance of hundreds of them creates a magic carpet of life.
  5. Parched or damp, this millimeter-thick layer of microbes and plants creates a protective living skin for Earth’s driest places.
